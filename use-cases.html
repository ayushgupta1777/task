General Dentistry

General dentistry is a branch of dental care that focuses on the overall oral health and hygiene of individuals. Dentists in general dentistry provide a wide range of preventive, diagnostic, and restorative services to address common dental issues. This includes regular check-ups, cleanings, X-rays, and treatments for cavities and gum disease. General dentists also educate patients on proper oral care practices to maintain optimal dental health. While they may refer patients to specialists for specific treatments, general dentists play a key role in promoting and preserving oral health for people of all ages.

01

Implants

Medical implants, crafted from biocompatible materials, are specialized devices inserted inside or on the body for therapeutic purposes. Examples include dental implants for tooth replacement, orthopedic implants like hip and knee replacements, and breast implants for augmentation or reconstruction. Cardiac implants such as pacemakers regulate heart rhythms, while neurostimulators manage pain and neurological disorders. Cochlear implants restore hearing, and intraocular lenses aid in vision restoration during cataract surgery. These devices are crucial in various medical fields, addressing specific health needs through carefully conducted surgical procedures.

02

Tooth Coloured Filling

Tooth-colored fillings, also known as composite fillings, are a popular and aesthetically pleasing alternative to traditional amalgam (silver) fillings. Composed of a mixture of plastic resin and finely ground glass particles, these fillings closely match the natural color of teeth, making them virtually indistinguishable. Dentists use tooth-colored fillings to repair cavities and restore the natural appearance of a tooth after decay removal. Besides their cosmetic appeal, composite fillings bond directly to the tooth structure, often requiring less removal of healthy tooth material compared to amalgam fillings. This type of filling is a common choice for those seeking a discreet and natural-looking solution for dental restoration.

03

Root Canal Treatment

Root canal treatment, also known as endodontic therapy, is a dental procedure designed to address issues within the pulp of a tooth. When the pulp, which contains nerves, blood vessels, and connective tissue, becomes infected or inflamed due to deep decay, trauma, or other factors, a root canal is performed to save the tooth. During the procedure, the dentist removes the infected or damaged pulp, cleans the root canals, and seals the space with a biocompatible material. This helps prevent further infection and preserves the tooth's structure. Following a root canal, a tooth is typically restored with a crown to ensure strength and functionality. Root canal treatment is crucial for alleviating pain, saving the natural tooth, and maintaining oral health.

04

Crowns And Bridges

Dental crowns are custom coverings that restore damaged teeth, providing strength and cosmetic improvement. Meanwhile, dental bridges replace missing teeth, utilizing artificial teeth anchored by crowns on adjacent natural teeth or implants. Both crowns and bridges are vital in dental restoration, ensuring enhanced function and aesthetics. Crowns protect weakened teeth after procedures like root canals, while bridges help bridge gaps caused by missing teeth, contributing to improved oral health and an aesthetically pleasing smile. These prosthetic devices play key roles in preserving natural teeth and restoring dental integrity.

05

Partial And Complete Dentures

Dental prosthetics play a vital role in restoring oral health and aesthetics, with partial dentures addressing gaps when some natural teeth remain, preventing issues and improving function. Meanwhile, complete dentures become essential when all teeth are missing, offering comprehensive oral restoration by providing a full set of artificial teeth on a customized base. Whether it's partial dentures with metal frameworks clasping onto existing teeth or complete dentures ensuring proper speech and chewing through suction or adhesives, both types contribute significantly to the well-being and confidence of individuals dealing with tooth loss, preserving oral function and enhancing smiles.

06

Scaling

Scaling, a fundamental dental procedure, is a preventive and therapeutic measure aimed at maintaining optimal oral health. During scaling, a dentist or dental hygienist uses specialized instruments to remove plaque, tartar (hardened plaque), and bacteria from the surfaces of teeth, particularly around the gumline and between teeth. This process helps prevent and treat conditions like gingivitis and periodontitis, reducing the risk of gum disease and promoting overall oral hygiene. Scaling is typically accompanied by polishing to smoothen the tooth surfaces and discourage plaque buildup. This routine dental maintenance procedure plays a crucial role in preventing more severe dental issues, ensuring healthier gums and teeth for individuals of all ages.

07

Specialised Child Dentistry

Specialized child dentistry, also known as pediatric dentistry, is a field of dental care focused on the oral health of children from infancy through adolescence. Pediatric dentists undergo additional training to address the unique dental needs and concerns of young patients. These specialists provide a range of services tailored to children, including preventive care such as cleanings, fluoride treatments, and sealants. They also monitor the development of teeth and jaws, address early orthodontic issues, and educate both children and parents on proper oral hygiene practices. Additionally, pediatric dentists are trained to handle the emotional and behavioral aspects of treating children, creating a positive and comfortable environment to foster a lifetime of good oral health habits.

08

Cavity Prevention Treatments

Cavity prevention treatments are essential in maintaining optimal oral health and preventing dental decay. Fluoride treatments represent a key strategy, where fluoride, a natural mineral, is applied to the teeth to strengthen enamel and make it more resistant to acid attacks from bacteria. Dental sealants are another preventive measure, involving the application of a thin protective coating to the chewing surfaces of molars to create a barrier against bacteria and debris. Regular dental cleanings and check-ups play a vital role in cavity prevention by removing plaque and tartar buildup. Additionally, practicing good oral hygiene at home, including proper brushing, flossing, and a balanced diet, significantly contributes to reducing the risk of cavities. These combined efforts create a comprehensive approach to cavity prevention, promoting long-term dental health and minimizing the need for restorative treatments.

09

Orthodontic treatment

Orthodontic treatment is a specialized dental discipline focused on diagnosing, preventing, and correcting misalignments or irregularities in the teeth and jaws. The most common form of orthodontic treatment involves the use of braces, which consist of brackets attached to the teeth connected by wires and bands. Braces apply controlled pressure to gradually move teeth into the desired positions, addressing issues such as crooked teeth, overcrowding, overbites, and underbites.

10

Aesthetic Dentistry

Aesthetic dentistry, or cosmetic dentistry, is dedicated to enhancing the visual appeal of teeth and smiles. Procedures within this field include teeth whitening, dental veneers, bonding, and gum contouring, all aimed at improving the shape, color, and overall aesthetics of teeth. Clear aligners, part of orthodontic treatments, also fall under aesthetic dentistry, contributing to a straighter smile. Dentists in this specialty focus on creating personalized treatment plans that not only address cosmetic concerns but also promote optimal oral health, ultimately boosting individuals' confidence and self-esteem through a more aesthetically pleasing smile.

11

Smile Designing

Smile designing is a comprehensive approach within cosmetic dentistry aimed at creating a harmonious and aesthetically pleasing smile tailored to an individual's unique facial features and preferences. This process involves a combination of cosmetic dental procedures such as teeth whitening, veneers, crowns, and gum contouring to address issues like discoloration, misalignment, or uneven gumlines. Dentists in smile designing work closely with patients to understand their desired outcomes and employ advanced digital imaging technology to plan and visualize the proposed changes. The goal is to not only enhance the appearance of the teeth but to create a balanced and natural-looking smile that complements the overall facial structure. Smile designing plays a significant role in boosting self-confidence and contributing to an individual's overall sense of well-being through the transformation of their smile.

12

Teeth Whitening

Teeth whitening is a popular cosmetic dental procedure that involves the application of a whitening agent, usually containing hydrogen peroxide, to break down stains on the tooth enamel. This treatment, available both in-office with specialized lights or lasers and through at-home kits with custom-fitted trays, effectively addresses discolorations caused by aging, consumption of staining substances, or tobacco use. While providing a non-invasive way to achieve a brighter smile, the longevity of results varies based on individual habits. Regular dental check-ups can help determine the most suitable whitening approach and maintain a whiter, more radiant smile over time.
